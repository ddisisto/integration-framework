## Grounding circularity: the distortion measure escape

The reviewer flagged that Claim 6 (grounding as rate-distortion) risks circularity: "grounding is preserving distortion-relevant structure" reduces to "grounding is grounding the right things" unless you say where the distortion measure comes from.

The escape: you never need a universal distortion measure. You need two independently specifiable measures and a way to compare them.

1. **The model's distortion measure** is determined by training objective + data. A text-only model's measure is "preserve whatever statistical structure in text helps predict the next token." Fully specified without reference to grounding.

2. **The task-specific distortion measure** is determined by the domain. Zaslavsky had CIELAB for color. Spatial reasoning has physical affordances. Medical diagnosis has clinical outcomes. Specifiable per domain, not universally.

3. **Grounding is the overlap** between these two. Does the model's training-induced measure preserve structure that matters under the task-specific measure? Measurable, not circular.

Xu et al. (2025) is this move in practice: text-only training recovers non-sensorimotor but not sensorimotor features. That's an empirical finding about where two independently defined distortion measures overlap and where they don't.

Key insight: **this is the same structure as the alignment argument** already in Section 5. Alignment = distortion measure mismatch between model and receiver. Grounding = specific instance where the receiver's measure includes structure (sensorimotor, causal, experiential) that may not be recoverable from text-only training. The circularity dissolves because neither measure is defined in terms of the other.

The equivalent move to Zaslavsky's CIELAB: you make it domain by domain, not universally. That's not a weakness — it's the right answer. Grounding is a family of domain-specific questions about distortion measure overlap, each empirically approachable.

### What to change in Section 4

The grounding-as-rate-distortion discussion needs to make this move explicitly: acknowledge the circularity risk, then show that the framework dissolves it by decomposing "the" distortion measure into two independently specifiable ones. Connect back to the alignment argument in Section 5 — same formal structure, different instance.
